  - "200g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)"
  - "120g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing"
  - "2 whole eggs"
  - "2 egg yolks"
  - "1/4 cup sugar"
  - "2 tbsp all-purpose flour"
  - "Pinch of salt"
  - "1 tsp vanilla extract"
  - "Cocoa powder for dusting"
  - "Vanilla ice cream for serving"
  - "Fresh raspberries for garnish"
instructions:
  - "Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Butter four 6-oz ramekins generously and dust with cocoa powder."
  - "Melt chocolate and butter together in a heatproof bowl over simmering water (or in 30-second microwave bursts), stirring until smooth. Let cool slightly."
  - "In a separate bowl, whisk together whole eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla until thick and pale, about 3 minutes."
  - "Fold the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture. Then fold in the flour and salt until just combined."
  - "Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins (they should be about 3/4 full)."
  - "Place ramekins on a baking sheet and bake for exactly 12-14 minutes. The edges should be set but the center should still jiggle."
  - "Let rest for 1 minute, then run a knife around the edges. Place a plate on top, flip, and gently lift the ramekin away."
  - "Serve immediately with a scoop of vanilla ice cream and fresh raspberries."

## About This Recipe

The molten chocolate lava cake is the ultimate showstopper that's actually easy to make. The secret is precise timing — even one minute too long means you'll lose that gorgeous liquid center.

## Tips

- **Do not overbake!** 12 minutes is usually perfect. The center should look barely set.
- You can prepare the batter ahead of time and refrigerate. Add 1-2 minutes to baking time if baking from cold.
- Use high-quality dark chocolate — it's the star of the show.

## Make Ahead

Fill the ramekins and refrigerate for up to 8 hours before baking. Add 1-2 minutes to the baking time when baking from chilled.
