One-Pot Chicken Tikka Masala

Priya Sharma Easy Serves 4 Prep: 15 min Cook: 35 min

A simplified weeknight version of the beloved Indian classic — creamy, spiced, and comforting.

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 35 min
Servings 4
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, 1 tsp garam masala, turmeric, cumin, and paprika. Add chicken pieces and marinate for at least 15 minutes (or overnight in the fridge for best results).
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Remove chicken from marinade (reserving the marinade) and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Set aside.
  3. In the same pot, melt butter. Add diced onion and cook until golden, about 8 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, reserved marinade, remaining garam masala, and sugar. Stir to combine.
  6. Return the chicken to the pot. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat and cook covered for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Stir in the heavy cream and simmer for 5 more minutes. Taste and adjust salt.
  8. Serve over basmati rice, garnished with fresh cilantro, with warm naan on the side.

About This Recipe

This is my busy-weeknight version of chicken tikka masala. It skips the traditional tandoor step but still delivers that rich, creamy, deeply spiced sauce we all love. One pot = less cleanup!

Tips

  • Marinate the chicken overnight for the most flavorful result.
  • Adjust the spice level by adding or reducing the paprika and garam masala.
  • For a lighter version, substitute coconut milk for the heavy cream.

Serving Suggestions

  • Warm garlic naan for scooping up the sauce
  • A side of cooling cucumber raita
  • Pickled red onions for a tangy crunch
