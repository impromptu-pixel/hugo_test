Fluffy Japanese Soufflé Pancakes

Yuki Tanaka Hard Serves 2 Prep: 15 min Cook: 20 min

Cloud-like, jiggly Japanese soufflé pancakes that are as fun to make as they are to eat.

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 20 min
Servings 2
Difficulty Hard

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Whisk together egg yolks, milk, and vanilla extract in a large bowl until smooth.
  2. Sift in the cake flour and baking powder. Mix until just combined — do not overmix.
  3. In a separate clean bowl, beat egg whites with cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add sugar and beat until stiff, glossy peaks form.
  4. Fold one-third of the meringue into the yolk mixture to lighten it. Then gently fold in the remaining meringue in two additions, being careful not to deflate the batter.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan over the lowest heat setting. Lightly butter the surface.
  6. Using a piping bag or large spoon, pipe/ scoop tall mounds of batter (about 3 inches wide and 2 inches tall).
  7. Add 1 tbsp of water to the pan, cover with a lid, and cook on low heat for 5-6 minutes.
  8. Carefully flip the pancakes, add another tbsp of water, cover, and cook for another 5-6 minutes until cooked through.
  9. Serve immediately — they deflate quickly! Top with maple syrup, fresh berries, and a dusting of powdered sugar.

About This Recipe

Soufflé pancakes became an internet sensation, and for good reason! These impossibly fluffy pancakes are a weekend morning treat. The key is patience — low heat and gentle handling.

Tips

  • Room temperature eggs work best for maximum volume.
  • Make sure your bowl for egg whites is completely clean and grease-free.
  • Don’t rush the cooking — low and slow is the way to go!
  • Serve immediately as they deflate within minutes.

Common Mistakes

  • Overmixing the batter (deflates the meringue)
  • Heat that’s too high (burns outside, raw inside)
  • Not using cream of tartar (stabilizes the egg whites)
