Chocolate Lava Cake

Jean-Pierre Dubois Medium Serves 4 Prep: 10 min Cook: 14 min

Impressive yet surprisingly simple molten chocolate cakes with a gooey center.

Prep Time 10 min
Cook Time 14 min
Servings 4
Difficulty Medium

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Butter four 6-oz ramekins generously and dust with cocoa powder.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together in a heatproof bowl over simmering water (or in 30-second microwave bursts), stirring until smooth. Let cool slightly.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together whole eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla until thick and pale, about 3 minutes.
  4. Fold the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture. Then fold in the flour and salt until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins (they should be about 3/4 full).
  6. Place ramekins on a baking sheet and bake for exactly 12-14 minutes. The edges should be set but the center should still jiggle.
  7. Let rest for 1 minute, then run a knife around the edges. Place a plate on top, flip, and gently lift the ramekin away.
  8. Serve immediately with a scoop of vanilla ice cream and fresh raspberries.

About This Recipe

The molten chocolate lava cake is the ultimate showstopper that’s actually easy to make. The secret is precise timing — even one minute too long means you’ll lose that gorgeous liquid center.

Tips

  • Do not overbake! 12 minutes is usually perfect. The center should look barely set.
  • You can prepare the batter ahead of time and refrigerate. Add 1-2 minutes to baking time if baking from cold.
  • Use high-quality dark chocolate — it’s the star of the show.

Make Ahead

Fill the ramekins and refrigerate for up to 8 hours before baking. Add 1-2 minutes to the baking time when baking from chilled.
